Growing up, I generally preferred building things to just straight up gaming. Of course, gamers would be quick to point out that I would have been building reflexes and other mental acuities by playing certain games, but that's a tad pedantic. I just found it more rewarding building things, from computers to Lego towns and such.

With Lego, not "Legos". Next you'll be telling me you like counting sheeps. You weird, weird peoples.

To that end, Maxis games were an absolute drug. A-Train, SimCity, SimPark and SimTowerâ€¦ all of these let us break away from the constraints of affordability and house space into what we could model and build on a computer. Cynics would roll their eyes at my borderline synergised paradigm language there, but it was really true.

And then there was The Sims, which took the macro level task of building a city or skyscraper and boiled it down to the family unit. I loved building futures for my little people, but the houses were far and away my favourite part. I'd even convinced my year 10 Design and Tech teacher to design a building mockup using The Sims before I proceeded to butcher it with cardboard and clay.

(For a brief period of time, I wanted to be an architect. Frank Lloyd Wright, Art Deco, 70s Brutalism and everything in between. Like all those other interests though, they took a back seat to that glowing, CRT-fronted 486SX).

That was a long introduction

I preface this arguably short announcement with all this nonsense to frame just how excited I am by this. With the release of The Sims 4 imminent, the legendary Mac game studio Aspyr have re-released The Sims 2 on the App Store, including most of the expansions pre-installed.

Reread that paragraph. Then look back at me. Then reread that paragraph. Then look back at me.

Having remembered the fun it took getting The Sims 1 and all its expansions working on an Intel Mac with all these PowerPC binaries and patches, and the QuickTime incompatibilities with various later versions of Mac OS X that somehow prevented a bunch of expansions working, this made me more excited than I care to admit.

A ready to play app bundle with most of the expansions all installed for us? YES PLEASE.

Of course, with TPG internet at our home, I had to download it from the office. I also had to free over 16GB of drive space on a 64GB MacBook Air before the App Store would allow me to download it. But there it was, a few hours later, shiny and ready to play.

For those used to the classic game, there are a couple of nice extras. Aspyr tells us they refactored the rendering engine for the game, meaning it does play halfway decent even on an 11 inch Air with integrated graphics. It can even be run in a window, so you can stil tweet your excitement from TweetBot while you play. They've done a really nice job.
