--- title: "Raspberry Pi Model A" slug: "raspberry-pi-model-a" date: "2015-03-20T11:54:00+11:00" categories: - Hardware tags: - rasperry-pi - reviews --- I just left a review of the Raspberry Pi Model A on the Tronixlabs site.

I bought this from Tronixlabs shortly after it became available. Wonderful little board and supplier :)

If you don't need all the IO and memory of a regular Pi, will be interacting with it remotely and/or space and power consumption are critical, the A may be your ticket.

The limited IO can be offset by utilising a serial console on the GPIO riser, freeing the lone USB port for other uses. You can wire your own with a MAX3232, which allows interfacing with the A's 3.3v serial with a regular 5v port.

Otherwise, there are plenty of after market integrated serial boards, though this does eat into the cost saving of getting the A over the B. Still, it still has the power and size advantages.
