--- title: "Only pay once, but then pay again" date: "2015-06-10T15:23:00+10:00" summary: "When one-time purchase means paying many times" categories: - Software tags: - licencing - atlassian - confluence ---

Atlassian's pricing page for Confluence lists the following server benefits:

Server Benefits

  • Host on your own hardware.
  • Extreme customization.
  • Perpetual license: one-time purchase.
  • Add-ons available from Atlassian Marketplace.
  • Complete application control.

A perpetual licence. Get Confluence with a "one-time purchase". Sounds delightfully simple, but alas it's more complicated. On their purchase page:

All software purchases include 12 months of maintenance. Maintenance entitles you to product support and software updates.

They then sell longer periods for maintenance.

This seems to be the new norm; sell something for a low price (or even give it away for free), then charge for ongoing support.

Is this the enterprise equivilent to mobile app stores giving away software with in-app purchases, or Adobe for end users with Creative Cloud?
