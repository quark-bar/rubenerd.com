--- title: "Seamonkey on retina screens" ---

I've had a Retina MacBook Pro for half a year now. Dangerously, the quality of the screen has gone from dazzling to normal; non-retina screens look awful now.

Most applications take advantage of the screen just fine, though there are a few holdouts. The latest versions of SeaMonkey still render their entire XUL interface at half the resolutuon, which results in jarringly bad text.

Open /Applications/SeaMonkey.app/Contents/Info.plist, and update the last lines to reflect this:

<key>NSSupportsAutomaticGraphicsSwitching</key>
<true/>
<key>NSPrincipalClass</key>
<string>GeckoNSApplication</string>

UI elements will still be pixelated, but at least text is legible again.
