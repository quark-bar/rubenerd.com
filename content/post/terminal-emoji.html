--- title: "Terminal Emoji" date: 2015-05-18T17:37:00+10:00 summary: "Some will hate this, but I find them useful" categories: - Software tags: - emoji - design - terminal ---

I never thought I'd see it, but we have real graphics in our terminals. Granted, they're fonts with emoji the OS may render as an image, but the point stands. Pontifications over how positive a development this is I leave as an exercise for the reader.

The Homebrew team include their namesake beverage in output. Some put bash burgers. Personally, I've started adding hot unicode beverages, which OS X renders as a lovely emoji cup of coffee: 

☕   Fluxing capacitors...
[STDOUT...]
☕   Building dependencies...
[STDOUT...]
☕   Grading zettai ryoiki...
[STDOUT...]
☕   Changes now LIVE. Have a nice day

These visual markers really stand out in a sea of output. I've yet to push them into prod for anything other than personal deployments though.
