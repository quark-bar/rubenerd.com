Since largely reverting Rubénerd to its original 2004 colour scheme, I figured there'd be people asking why this site looks like this.

Since 2010, I aimed to make Rubénerd more contemporary, tasteful, classy; whatever the cool people do. I switched to a blazing white theme, tried centering stuff, removed borders, rendering my fonts thinner, and generally made everything look cookie-cutter, generic, and forgettable.

Then I realised something. There are enough of those sites out there. I'd make my own corner of the net as unique as dishwater; which I suppose is unique insofar as the unique combination of suds and food scraps would be unlikely to be reproducable. So it was as unique as... something that isn't.

Screw that.

So in the end, I switched back. Is this theme contemporary, tasteful and classy? Heck no. But it's mine. I can look at it with eye floaters. As someone rapidly approaching his thirties, I find it soothing on my overworked eyes. It's less of a shock going from the dark Vim terminal windows I use to write it, to the published site.

(For a while, I even reverted back to XHTML+RDFa, before common sense prevailed. I do still prefer RDF syntax to needlessly-duplicated microdata, but that's for another discussion).

I've also never loaded heavy JavaScript libraries or external fonts, and I'm not about to. That's my definition of good taste.
